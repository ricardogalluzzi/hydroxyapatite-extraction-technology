## Introduction
This repository documents the scientific and technological development of a novel process for extracting hydroxyapatite from fish scales using ultrasound-assisted alkaline hydrolysis. The technology originated from practical experience with ultrasound systems and evolved into a patented process, which is now supported by peer-reviewed scientific publications, open datasets, and comprehensive technical documentation.

## Mission
To demonstrate the feasibility of converting fish-processing residues into high-value-added biogenic hydroxyapatite through a sustainable, efficient, and patented technological process.

## Technological Background
The development of this technology originated from hands-on experience with industrial ultrasound systems and the realization that fish scales, which are commonly discarded as environmental waste, contain mineral phases with high potential for hydroxyapatite production. 

The combination of alkaline hydrolysis and ultrasonic assistance was investigated as a viable route to obtain pure biogenic hydroxyapatite, simultaneously contributing to the upcycling and valorization of fish-processing residues. Due to its novelty and inventive step, the resulting process successfully achieved patent protection in Brazil.
